Introduction

A holder wallet is a digital wallet that stores and manages your verifiable credentials. It is a key component of the OnePass ecosystem, as it allows you to store, manage, and present your credentials in a secure and user-friendly way.

When choosing a holder wallet, there are several factors to consider, including security, privacy, usability, and interoperability. In this guide, we will explore the key features to look for in a holder wallet and provide recommendations for popular wallets that are compatible with OnePass.

Key Features of a Holder Wallet

1. Security

Security is a critical aspect of any holder wallet. Look for wallets that offer robust encryption, biometric authentication, and secure storage mechanisms to protect your credentials from unauthorized access.

2. Privacy

Privacy is another essential consideration when choosing a holder wallet. Ensure that the wallet gives you full control over your data and allows you to selectively disclose information without compromising your privacy.

3. Usability

A user-friendly interface and intuitive design are key factors in selecting a holder wallet. Choose a wallet that is easy to navigate, supports multiple languages, and provides clear instructions on how to manage your credentials.

4. Interoperability

Interoperability is crucial for seamless integration with OnePass and other decentralized identity platforms. Select a wallet that supports industry standards such as W3C’s Verifiable Credentials and Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs) to ensure compatibility with various services.

5. Compatibility

Ensure that the holder wallet you choose is compatible with OnePass and other verifiable credential issuers. Check the list of supported wallets on the OnePass website to find wallets that meet the platform’s requirements.

Compatible Holder Wallets

OnePass is compatible with several trusted holder wallets, with the Talao Wallet  and Walt.id Wallet  being two of the most recommended options. These wallets have been thoroughly tested with our platform and offer robust security features along with user-friendly interfaces. Below, we’ll explore the unique features of each wallet and provide step-by-step setup instructions for use with OnePass.

1. Talao Wallet

The Talao Wallet  is a non-custodial mobile wallet that supports verifiable credentials and decentralized identity. It offers a user-friendly interface, strong security features, and seamless integration with OnePass. To download the Talao Wallet:

  1. Search for the “Talao” wallet in the app store on your mobile device (App Store or Google Play) and install the app.
  2. Click the “Create a digital wallet” button.
  3. Select your preferred authentication method and read and accept the terms and conditions.
  4. Click the “Start” button.
  5. Go to the “Settings” tab of your wallet and click “Wallet profiles.”
  6. Choose the option “European Blockchain Services Infrastructure (EBSI v4.0)” to set up your wallet for use with OnePass.
Links:
  • Talao website 
  • IOS App Store: Talao Wallet 
  • Google Play Store: Talao Wallet 

2. Walt.id Wallet

The Walt.id Wallet  is a custodial web-based wallet that offers secure storage and management of verifiable credentials. It provides a reliable solution for storing and presenting your credentials. To use the Walt.id Wallet:

  1. Open the Walt.id Web Wallet  in your browser.
  2. Register for an account and follow the on-screen instructions to set up your wallet.
Links:
  • Walt.id Web Wallet 

For optimal user experience, we recommend using the Walt.id Wallet as a Progressive Web App (PWA) on your mobile device. This provides a more app-like experience and easier access to your credentials.

To add the wallet to your home screen:

iOS Safari

  1. Visit wallet.walt.id
  2. Tap the share button (box with arrow)
  3. Scroll down and tap “Add to Home Screen”
  4. Tap “Add” to confirm

Android Chrome

  1. Visit wallet.walt.id
  2. Tap the menu (three dots)
  3. Select “Add to Home Screen”
  4. Tap “Add” to confirm

Using the wallet on mobile offers several advantages:

  • Quick access to your credentials
  • Better camera integration for QR code scanning
  • More convenient authentication using biometrics
  • Native sharing features for credential presentation

Conclusion

Choosing the right holder wallet is essential for managing your verifiable credentials effectively. By considering factors such as security, privacy, usability, interoperability, and compatibility, you can select a wallet that meets your needs and integrates seamlessly with OnePass. Explore the recommended wallets mentioned in this guide and start securely storing and presenting your credentials today.

If you have any questions or need assistance with choosing a holder wallet, feel free to reach out to the OnePass support team for guidance and support. We are here to help you navigate the world of decentralized identity and verifiable credentials.
